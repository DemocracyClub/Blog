{% filter markdown %} ## Elections Assistant - Devolved Parliaments Focus Democracy Club is a non-partisan community interest company which builds online voter information tools for UK elections. Our vision is of a country with the digital foundations to support everyone’s participation in democratic life. We’re a small, distributed team who work openly; our partners include The Electoral Commission, local authorities, and a wide range of media companies and similar organisations. 2021 will see one of the largest and most complex sets of elections the UK has ever experienced: local elections in England; Police and Crime Commissioners in England and Wales; the Greater London Authority; and the devolved parliaments of Scotland and Wales. In order to ensure that we achieve the greatest impact during these elections, we’re seeking a fixed-term Elections Assistant to help with the administration and dissemination of our voter information tools. Although this role will touch upon all regions of the UK, it will involve a specific focus on the Scottish Parliament and Welsh Parliament/Senedd Cymru. This non-technical role would suit someone with an enthusiasm for democracy and democratic participation, and a commitment to getting the details right. We don’t have an ideal candidate in mind and are happy to modify the role to suit individual interests/specialisms, so if you think this sounds like your sort of thing please don’t hesitate to get in touch! ### Main responsibilities: * Help us partner with every English, Welsh and Scottish local authority to obtain data for [our polling station finder](https://wheredoivote.co.uk/). This involves using CRM software to track progress and deal with problems and queries as they arise. * Contribute to, manage, and monitor [our candidates database](https://candidates.democracyclub.org.uk/), and help develop voter information resources to be displayed on our popular [candidate look-up site](https://whocanivotefor.co.uk/). * Proactively seek out potential partners and data re-users: local journalists and newspapers; universities; community organisations (eg. encourage them to use our tools on their website). * Build awareness of Democracy Club and its services, especially across Wales/Scotland, both for May 2021 and in anticipation of the local elections in 2022. * Contribute to Democracy Club’s wider elections output and partnership work. To get a better feel for what we do and what the role will involve, take a scroll down our blog and have a read of [our report on the 2019 general election](https://democracyclub.org.uk/report_2019_general_election/). ###Requirements Essential * Good understanding of UK politics, especially the electoral systems and structure of local/devolved government * Excellent written and spoken communication skills * Organised with a good attention to detail (we use a lot of spreadsheets!) * Fast and adaptable learner, especially when it comes to using new software * Comfortable working independently and taking initiative to push projects forward * Thoroughly non-partisan - must not be a member of a political party, have stood for or held elected office in the UK, or been involved in any partisan political campaign during the last two years Desirable * Experience of Scottish/Welsh third sector or government ### Benefits * A chance to immerse yourself in civic technology/ tech for good * A unique perspective on UK electoral administration * £20,000 pro rata salary ### Location Based anywhere in the UK with a good internet connection, but you’ll ideally have close connections with Wales or Scotland ### When Mid-January to 30 May 2021. This is for the elections to be held on 6 May. ### Hours Part-time, flexible hours. This is negotiable but we anticipate 16-26 hours a week ### Closing date midnight 22nd November 2020 We hope to hold interviews in early December ### Applying Your CV/resume should be no longer than one side of A4, and your cover letter need not be longer than 400 words. Please make sure you have also answered the question on the application form. If you have any questions about this role please get in touch with Peter at [peter.keeling@democracyclub.org.uk](mailto:peter.keeling@democracyclub.org.uk).
